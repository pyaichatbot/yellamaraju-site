## Why Async Matters

Enterprise workflows often exceed one request window. Treat agent execution as a job lifecycle, not a single synchronous call.

## Operational Contract

- Explicit job states and transitions
- Polling endpoints with backoff
- Cancellation and compensation behavior
- SLA alerts for stuck jobs
- Retention and privacy controls for job traces

<RoleCallout role="dev">
Expose stable job IDs and status APIs. Frontend should never infer state from logs or timing assumptions.
</RoleCallout>

<RoleCallout role="qa">
Test race conditions: cancel-while-running, retry-after-timeout, and duplicate client polling.
</RoleCallout>

<RoleCallout role="pm">
Define user-facing status language and escalation paths when jobs exceed expected duration.
</RoleCallout>

<Callout type="warning" title="Production Gotcha">
Without cancellation semantics, operators end up with orphaned workflows that still execute side effects after users abandon the task.
</Callout>
